SUPER BIKE

To be eligible for superbike racing, a manufacturer must first homologate the model and produce the required number of roadgoing machines. The bike must maintain the same profile as its roadgoing counterpart, with the same overall appearance from the front, rear, and sides. The frame cannot be modified, although teams can modify certain elements like suspensions, brakes, swingarm, and wheel size. Superbike racing motorcycles must have four-stroke engines with specific displacement limits depending on the number of cylinders. Twins must be between 850 cc and 1200 cc, while four-cylinder machines must be between 750 cc and 1000 cc.
